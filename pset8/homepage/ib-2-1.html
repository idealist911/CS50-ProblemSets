Kinematics

Kinematics is a study on describing motion. Although we know concepts such as distance and speed intuitively, kinematics provide the rigor to quantify those concepts, so that we can quantitatively explain a phenomenon by observing the motion of the objects involved.

Terminologies

Position

To be able to tell where an object is, we need to know not just how far away it is, but also its direction.

Hence, position is a vector quantity. We often denote it by ${\vec{x}}$.

Displacement

Displacement is the change in position, that is, $${\vec{s} = \Delta\vec{x} = \vec{x}_f - \vec{x}_i\,,}$$ where ${\vec{x}_i}$ and ${\vec{x}_f}$ are the initial and final positions respectively.

Displacment adds up as such: Suppose one goes from the initial position to some mid position and on to the final position, $${\vec{s} = \vec{s}_1 + \vec{s}_2 = (\vec{x}_\mathrm{mid} - \vec{x}_i) + (\vec{x}_f - \vec{x}_\mathrm{mid}) = \vec{x}_f - \vec{x}_i\,.}$$

Distance

Distance is the length of the path taken.

Although indistinguishable from displacement in non-scientific communities, there is a subtle but crucial difference: distance can only accumulate, but displacement could decrease (if you walk back to your initial position). Suppose one goes in a straight line from the initial position to some mid position and in a straight line on to the final position, $${D = \vec{D}_1 + \vec{D}_2 = |\vec{x}_\mathrm{mid} - \vec{x}_i| + |\vec{x}_f - \vec{x}_\mathrm{mid}|\,.}$$ If the path is not straight, one would have to break the path down into pieces so small that each piece looks like a straight line. Hence, after running one round on the track, you have covered a distance of 400 m, but your displacement is zero.

Distance is a scalar quantity.
